K Acid

2-Naphthylamine-3,6,8-trisulfonic acid, also known as “K acid,” is an organic compound with the chemical formula C10H10N2O6S3. It is a trisulfonic acid derivative of 2-naphthylamine.

Applications & Uses :

It is used as a dye intermediate. It is used to produce a variety of dyes, pigments, and other colorants for a wide range of applications such as textile, leather, and paper industries. K acid is known for its excellent light fastness and high tinting strength.

Storage Notes :

Store K Acid in a tightly sealed container in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat. Avoid contact with incompatible chemicals, ensure ventilation, use proper handling gear, label containers clearly, have first aid measures in place, and maintain security.

2. Environment: Keep in a cool, dry place away from sunlight and heat.
3. Compatibility: Avoid storing near incompatible chemicals.
4. Ventilation: Ensure adequate ventilation to prevent gas buildup.
5. Handling: Use protective gear to avoid skin or eye contact.
6. Labeling: Clearly label containers with hazard warnings.
7. First Aid: Have a first aid kit and procedures in place.
8. Security: Store securely to prevent unauthorized access.
